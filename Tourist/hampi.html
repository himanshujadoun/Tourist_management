Hampi

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the southern Indian state of Karnataka, is a mesmerizing destination that transports visitors back in time. This ancient city was once the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire and is now a captivating archaeological site. Its stunning ruins, intricate stone carvings, and temple are a testament to a bygone era of grandeur and architectural excellence. The breathtaking landscape, with boulder-strewn terrain and lush greenery, adds to the allure. Tourists can explore the Virupaksha Temple, the Royal Enclosure, and the awe-inspiring Vittala Temple. Hampi's rich history, combined with its natural beauty, makes nature lovers alike.
